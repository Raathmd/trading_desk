TRAMMO GENERAL TERMS AND CONDITIONS (CFR AND CIF SALES)

1. PRODUCT AND SPECIFICATIONS

Product: Anhydrous Ammonia
Product Specifications:
  - Purity: minimum 99.5%
  - Water: maximum 0.3%
  - Oil: maximum 5 ppm

2. QUANTITY

Quantity: 20,000 metric tons +/- 5% more or less at vessel's option.

3. INCOTERMS

INCOTERMS 2020: CFR Jorf Lasfar, Morocco.

4. ORIGIN

Origin: Any origin. Seller has the right but not the obligation to source
from alternate origin provided the product meets the agreed specifications.

5. PRICE

Price: US $395.00 per metric ton CFR discharge port.

6. PORT(S) OF DISCHARGE

Port(s) of Discharge: One safe port, one safe berth, Jorf Lasfar, Morocco.
Draft guarantee of 14 meters at discharge berth.

7. ARRIVAL DATES

Arrival Dates: During the period March 20 - April 10, 2026.
Seller to provide 10 days prior notice of vessel nomination.

8. PAYMENT

Payment: By irrevocable letter of credit, payable at sight, opened in
Seller's favor within 10 days of contract date. Payment within 30 days
from bill of lading date.

9. LAYTIME AND DEMURRAGE

Discharge Rate and Demurrage: Discharge rate of 500 mt per hour. Laytime for
discharge shall be calculated based on B/L quantity divided by the agreed
discharge rate, all weather days, Sundays and holidays included.
Demurrage: $30,000 per day or pro rata for any fraction thereof. Demurrage
claims to be presented within 90 calendar days from date of completion of
discharge.

10. CHARTER PARTY

The Charter Party in ASBATANKVOY form is incorporated by reference.
Seller is deemed "owner" and Buyer is deemed "charterer" for the purpose of
this contract.

11. NOTICE OF READINESS

Notice of Readiness: Master to tender NOR upon arrival at or off discharge
port. Laytime shall commence 6 hours after NOR is tendered, or when vessel
is all fast at berth, whichever first occurs.

12. VESSEL CLASSIFICATION AND ELIGIBILITY

Vessel Classification and Eligibility: Vessel must be classified by an IACS
member society. P&I coverage must be provided by an International Group club.
ISPS Code compliance is mandatory.

13. INSPECTION

Inspection: Seller shall appoint an independent inspector at loading port.
Inspector's determination of quality and quantity shall be final and binding
for purposes of payment. Cost of inspection at loading port for seller's
account.

14. INSURANCE

Insurance: Seller shall arrange cargo insurance on an all risk basis for not
less than the contract price plus ten percent (110% CIF). War strike riot
coverage shall be included.

15. LETTER OF INDEMNITY

Letter of Indemnity: Seller may issue a LOI in accordance with the P&I Club's
recommended wording to enable discharge without presentation of an original
Bill of Lading if original documents are delayed.

16. TAXES, FEES AND DUES

Taxes, Fees and Dues: Any import taxes, fees, dues or VAT applicable at the
discharge port shall be added to the sale price and paid by Buyer. Export taxes
at origin shall be for Seller's account.

17. WAR RISK

War Risk: Additional premiums assessed by Joint War Committee listed areas
shall be for Buyer's account. In the event of Main Shipping Routes Closure,
either party may request renegotiation of delivery terms.

18. FORCE MAJEURE

Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for non-performance due to Force
Majeure. The affected party shall notify the other within 5 days notice of the
event. Force Majeure events do not suspend demurrage obligations.

19. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

Event of Default: If a party commits a material breach and fails to remedy
within 48 hours of notice, the other party may terminate. Late payments bear
interest at 2% per annum above EURIBOR.

Penalty for late delivery: If Seller fails to deliver within the agreed arrival
window, Seller shall pay Buyer $22.00 per metric ton on the delayed volume.

20. EXCLUSION OF WARRANTIES

Disclaimer of Warranties: SELLER MAKES NO WARRANTY, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED,
INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO ANY WARRANTY OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS
FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

21. CLAIMS

Claims: Written notice of any claim must be given within 15 days of completion
of discharge. Any claim shall be absolutely barred unless arbitration is
commenced within one year from the date of discharge.

22. GOVERNING LAW AND ARBITRATION

Governing Law: English law.
Arbitration: All disputes shall be referred to arbitration in London in
accordance with LMAA terms.

23. NOTICES

Notices: Any notice hereunder shall be deemed to be given when sent by courier,
fax, or e-mail to the party at its registered address.

24. MISCELLANEOUS

Miscellaneous: This Entire Agreement supersedes all prior agreements.
Modifications must be in writing. No Waiver shall be construed as a continuing

Sale Contract for Barge, Rail and Trucks

Contract No.: TRAMMO-DS-2026-0109
Date: January 5, 2026
Effective: January 1, 2026
Term: January 1, 2026 through December 31, 2026

BETWEEN:
Trammo, Inc. — Ammonia Division ("Seller")
AND:
Koch Fertilizer LLC — Memphis Operations ("Buyer")

TRAMMO GENERAL TERMS AND CONDITIONS (SALES)

1. PRODUCT AND SPECIFICATIONS

Product: Anhydrous Ammonia
Specifications: Minimum purity 99.5%, water content maximum 0.5%,
oil content maximum 5 ppm.

2. QUANTITY

Quarterly Quantity: 4,000 metric tons per quarter +/- 10% more or less
at buyer's option. Annual total: 16,000 metric tons. Individual barge lots
of approximately 1,500 metric tons each. Minimum of two (2) barge lots
per quarter.

3. INCOTERMS

INCOTERMS 2020: FOB Seller's barge loading terminal, Donaldsonville, Louisiana,
or FOB Geismar, Louisiana, at Seller's option.

4. PRICE

Price: US $390.00 per metric ton FOB barge. Price subject to quarterly
adjustment linked to NOLA barge index published by Fertecon. Price floor
of $355.00 per metric ton.

5. SHIPMENTS AND NOMINATIONS

Shipments / nominations: Buyer shall nominate individual barge lots with
7 days prior notice. Buyer shall provide quarterly nomination 15 days
before start of each quarter.

6. PAYMENT

Payment: By telegraphic transfer within 20 days from bill of lading date.
Late payment interest at 1.5% per annum above SOFR.

7. INSPECTION AND QUANTITY DETERMINATION

Inspection: Bill of lading quantity shall be final and binding for barge
shipments. Certified shore tank gauging at Seller's terminal.

8. LAYTIME AND DEMURRAGE

Laytime: Barge laytime shall be 30 running hours from time of NOR.
Demurrage: $7,000 per day or pro rata for barge demurrage. Demurrage
claims must be submitted within 60 calendar days from completion of
loading.

9. FORCE MAJEURE

Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for delays caused by Force
Majeure events including but not limited to: floods, hurricanes, lock
closures, river stage below 9 feet at Cairo gauge, ice conditions,
government orders, labor disputes, or equipment failure beyond reasonable
control. Notice within 3 days. River stage below 9 feet at Cairo gauge
or ice closure of the Upper Mississippi shall entitle either party to
suspend barge deliveries without penalty.

10. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

Event of Default: Material default not remedied within 48 hours of notice
entitles termination. Interest at 1.5% per annum on late payments. Mutual
right of setoff and netting is preserved.

Penalty for volume shortfall: If Seller fails to deliver the minimum
quarterly quantity (net of tolerance), Seller shall pay Buyer $10.00 per
metric ton on the shortfall volume. If Buyer fails to accept the minimum
quarterly quantity, Buyer shall pay Seller $8.00 per metric ton on the
shortfall.

11. DISCLAIMER OF WARRANTIES

Disclaimer of Warranties: SELLER MAKES NO WARRANTY OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS
OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY.

12. CLAIMS

Claims: Written notice of claim within 30 days of delivery. Claims shall
be absolutely barred unless action is commenced within one year from date
of delivery.

13. GOVERNING LAW AND ARBITRATION

Governing Law: Laws of the State of New York shall govern this contract.
Arbitration: Disputes shall be settled by arbitration under SMA rules
in New York.

14. NOTICES

Notices: Notices deemed to be given when sent by courier, fax, or e-mail.

15. MISCELLANEOUS

Miscellaneous: Entire Agreement of the parties. Modifications in writing
only. No Waiver of breach implies waiver of future breach. Assignment
requires consent. Severability applies.

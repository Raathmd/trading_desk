ANHYDROUS AMMONIA SALES CONTRACT

Contract No.: TRAMMO-LTS-2026-0106
Date: December 1, 2025
Effective: January 1, 2026
Term: January 1, 2026 through December 31, 2027

BETWEEN:
Trammo SAS ("Seller")
AND:
Indian Farmers Fertiliser Cooperative Limited (IFFCO) ("Buyer")

LONG TERM CFR AMMONIA SALES AGREEMENT

1. PRODUCT AND SPECIFICATIONS

Product: Anhydrous Ammonia (NH3)
Specifications: Purity minimum 99.7%, water maximum 0.2%, oil maximum 5 ppm.
Temperature at loading: minus 33 degrees Celsius.

2. QUANTITY

Annual Quantity: 120,000 metric tons per year +/- 10% more or less at
buyer's option. Individual shipment size: approximately 35,000 metric tons
per shipment +/- 5% at vessel's option.

Shipping tolerance: Each shipment shall be 35,000 metric tons +/- 5%.

3. INCOTERMS

INCOTERMS 2020: CFR Kandla, India.

4. SHIPMENTS AND NOMINATIONS

Shipments / nominations: Buyer shall nominate quantities quarterly. Within
each quarter, nominations shall be spread across the months with at least
one cargo per month. Nominations and Notices: Buyer shall provide nomination
for each quarter no later than 45 days prior to the start of the quarter.
Individual vessel nominations require 25 days prior notice.
Arrival Dates: As nominated, during windows agreed for each quarter.

5. PRICE

Sale Price: The price shall be the average of Fertecon and FMB CFR India
ammonia assessments for the month preceding delivery, plus a premium of
$8.00 per metric ton.

6. PAYMENT

Payment: By irrevocable letter of credit opened in Seller's favor at a first
class international bank. Payment within 45 days from bill of lading date
against presentation of required documents.

7. LAYTIME AND DEMURRAGE

Discharge Rate and Demurrage: Discharge rate 450 mt per hour. Laytime
calculated as B/L quantity divided by discharge rate, all weather days,
Sundays and holidays included.
Demurrage: $36,000 per day or pro rata for any fraction thereof.
Claims within 90 calendar days from completion of discharge.

8. CHARTER PARTY

ASBATANKVOY Charter Party form is incorporated by reference herein. Seller is
deemed as "owner" and Buyer as "charterer."

9. NOTICE OF READINESS

Notice of Readiness: Master to tender NOR upon arrival at or off discharge
port. Laytime shall commence 6 hours after NOR is tendered, or when vessel
is all fast at berth, whichever first occurs.

10. VESSEL CLASSIFICATION AND ELIGIBILITY

Vessel Classification and Eligibility: Vessel must be classified by an IACS
member society. P&I coverage must be provided by an International Group club.
ISPS Code compliance is mandatory. Vessel age shall not exceed 25 years.

11. INSPECTION

Inspection: Independent inspector jointly appointed by Buyer and Seller at
loading port. Inspector's certificate shall be final and binding for quantity
and quality determination. Costs shared equally between the parties.

12. INSURANCE

Insurance: Seller shall arrange cargo insurance on an all risk basis for not
less than 110% CIF value. War strike riot coverage included.

13. LETTER OF INDEMNITY

LOI: Seller may deliver cargo without presentation of an original Bill of
Lading upon issuance of a Letter of Indemnity in P&I Club standard form.

14. TAXES, FEES AND DUES

Taxes, Fees and Dues: Import duties, VAT, and all taxes at destination shall
be added to the sale price. Export taxes at origin are for Seller's account.
Buyer is responsible for all import duty responsibility and customs clearance.

15. COMPLIANCE WITH EXPORT AND IMPORT LAWS

Compliance with Import Laws: Import of ammonia into India is subject to
applicable regulations. REACH registration maintained where applicable.
Safety Data Sheet provided annually.

16. WAR RISK

War Risk: Costs arising from transit through Joint War Committee listed areas
shall be for Buyer's account. In the event of Main Shipping Routes Closure
affecting the agreed route, either party may request alternate routing or
suspension of deliveries. Suez Canal transit costs for Seller's account.

17. FORCE MAJEURE

Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform due to
Force Majeure events beyond reasonable control. The affected party must notify
the other within 7 days notice. If Force Majeure continues for more than 90
days, either party may cancel the affected shipments.

18. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

Event of Default: Material breach not remedied within 48 hours of notice
entitles termination of the entire agreement. Interest on overdue payments
at 2% per annum above SOFR. Right of setoff and netting under this and
all related agreements is fully preserved.

Penalty for late delivery: If Seller fails to deliver within the nominated
arrival window, Seller shall pay Buyer $15.00 per metric ton on the
delayed volume as liquidated damages.

Penalty for volume shortfall: If Seller delivers less than the minimum annual
quantity (net of tolerance), Seller shall pay Buyer $12.00 per metric ton
on the shortfall volume.

19. EXCLUSION OF WARRANTIES

Disclaimer of Warranties: SELLER MAKES NO WARRANTY, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED,
INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR PURPOSE.

20. CLAIMS

Claims: Written notice of any claim within 15 days of discharge completion.
Claims absolutely barred unless arbitration commenced within one year.

21. GOVERNING LAW AND ARBITRATION

Governing Law: English law shall apply.
Arbitration: All disputes under LMAA terms in London.

22. NOTICES

Notices: All notices deemed to be given when delivered by courier, fax,
or e-mail to registered addresses.

23. MISCELLANEOUS

Miscellaneous: This constitutes the Entire Agreement. Modifications in
writing only. Waiver of one breach not a continuing waiver. Assignment
with prior written consent. Severability of invalid provisions.

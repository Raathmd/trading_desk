LONG TERM CFR AMMONIA SALES AGREEMENT

1. PRODUCT AND SPECIFICATIONS

Product: Anhydrous Ammonia (NH3)
Specifications: Purity minimum 99.5%, water maximum 0.2%, oil maximum 5 ppm.
Temperature at loading: minus 33 degrees Celsius.

2. QUANTITY

Annual Quantity: 100,000 metric tons per year +/- 5% more or less at
buyer's option. Individual shipment size: approximately 25,000 metric tons
per shipment +/- 5% at vessel's option.

Shipping tolerance: Each shipment shall be 25,000 metric tons +/- 5%.
Minimum of four (4) cargoes per contract year.

3. INCOTERMS

INCOTERMS 2020: CFR Tampa, Florida.

4. SHIPMENTS AND NOMINATIONS

Shipments / nominations: Buyer shall nominate quantities quarterly. Within
each quarter, nominations shall be spread as evenly as practicable across
the months. Nominations and Notices: Buyer shall provide nomination for each
quarter no later than 30 days prior to the start of the quarter. Individual
vessel nominations require 15 days prior notice.
Arrival Dates: As nominated, during windows agreed for each quarter.

5. PRICE

Sale Price: US $425.00 per metric ton CFR Tampa for Year 1. Price for Year 2
will be based on the average of Fertecon and FMB Tampa CFR assessments for
the month preceding delivery, with a floor of $390.00 per metric ton.

6. PAYMENT

Payment: By irrevocable letter of credit, payable at sight, or by telegraphic
transfer within 30 days from bill of lading date if creditworthy counterparty.

7. LAYTIME AND DEMURRAGE

Discharge Rate and Demurrage: Discharge rate 500 mt per hour. Laytime
calculated as B/L quantity divided by discharge rate.
Demurrage: $32,000 per day or pro rata for any fraction thereof.
Claims within 90 calendar days from completion of discharge.

8. CHARTER PARTY

ASBATANKVOY Charter Party form is incorporated by reference herein. Seller is
deemed as "owner" and Buyer as "charterer."

9. NOTICE OF READINESS

Notice of Readiness: Master to tender NOR upon arrival at or off discharge
port. Laytime shall commence 6 hours after NOR is tendered, or when vessel
is all fast at berth, whichever first occurs.

10. PRESENTATION AND COOL DOWN

Presentation of vessel cargo tanks shall be suitable for carrying ammonia.
Gassing and purging of cargo tanks using ammonia shall be for seller's account.
Time used for cool down shall count as laytime.

11. VESSEL CLASSIFICATION AND ELIGIBILITY

Vessel Classification and Eligibility: Vessel must be classified by an IACS
member society. P&I coverage must be provided by an International Group club.
ISPS Code compliance is mandatory. MTSA and USCG requirements must be met.

12. INSPECTION

Inspection: Seller shall appoint an independent inspector at loading port.
Inspector's determination of quality and quantity shall be final and binding
for purposes of payment. Cost of inspection at loading port for seller's
account.

13. INSURANCE

Insurance: Seller shall arrange cargo insurance on an all risk basis for not
less than the contract price plus ten percent (110% CIF). War strike riot
coverage shall be included.

14. LETTER OF INDEMNITY

Letter of Indemnity: Seller may issue a LOI in accordance with the P&I Club's
recommended wording to enable discharge without presentation of an original
Bill of Lading.

15. TAXES, FEES AND DUES

Taxes, Fees and Dues: Any import taxes, fees, dues or VAT applicable at the
discharge port shall be added to the sale price and paid by Buyer. Export taxes
at origin shall be for Seller's account.

16. COMPLIANCE WITH EXPORT AND IMPORT LAWS

Compliance with Export and Import Laws: The purchase and sale of the product is
not prohibited under applicable law. REACH registration is in effect. Seller
shall provide a Safety Data Sheet to Buyer prior to first delivery.

17. WAR RISK

War Risk: Additional premiums assessed by Joint War Committee listed areas shall
be for Buyer's account. In the event of Main Shipping Routes Closure, either
party may request renegotiation of delivery terms.

18. FORCE MAJEURE

Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for non-performance due to Force
Majeure. The affected party shall notify the other within 5 days notice of the
event. If Force Majeure continues for more than 60 days, Buyer may cancel the
affected shipments and source from alternate suppliers.

19. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

Event of Default: If a party commits a material breach and fails to remedy within
48 hours of notice, the other party may terminate. Late payments bear interest
at 2.5% per annum above SOFR. Each party retains its right of setoff and netting.

Penalty for late delivery: If Seller fails to deliver a nominated cargo within
the agreed arrival window, Seller shall pay Buyer $20.00 per metric ton on
the delayed cargo volume. Penalty capped at the freight differential between
original and substitute sourcing.

Penalty for volume shortfall: If Seller fails to deliver the minimum annual
quantity (net of tolerance), Seller shall pay Buyer $18.00 per metric ton
on the shortfall volume.

20. EXCLUSION OF WARRANTIES

Disclaimer of Warranties: SELLER MAKES NO WARRANTY, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED,
INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO ANY WARRANTY OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS
FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

21. CLAIMS

Claims: Written notice of any claim must be given within 15 days of completion
of discharge. Any claim shall be absolutely barred unless arbitration is
commenced within one year from the date of discharge.

22. GOVERNING LAW AND ARBITRATION

Governing Law: English law.
Arbitration: All disputes shall be referred to arbitration in London in
accordance with LMAA terms.

23. NOTICES

Notices: Any notice hereunder shall be deemed to be given when sent by courier,
fax, or e-mail to the party at its registered address.

24. MISCELLANEOUS

Miscellaneous: This Entire Agreement supersedes all prior agreements.
Modifications must be in writing. No Waiver shall be construed as a continuing
waiver. Assignment requires prior written consent. Severability applies.

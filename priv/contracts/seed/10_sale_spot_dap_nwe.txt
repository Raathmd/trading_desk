TRAMMO GENERAL TERMS AND CONDITIONS (DAP SALES)

1. PRODUCT AND SPECIFICATIONS

Product Specifications: Anhydrous Ammonia (NH3)
  Purity: minimum 99.8%
  Water: maximum 0.15%
  Oil: maximum 3 ppm

2. QUANTITY

Quantity: 15,000 metric tons +/- 3% more or less at buyer's option.

3. INCOTERMS

INCOTERMS 2020: DAP Ludwigshafen, Germany.

4. ORIGIN

Origin: Any origin acceptable to terminal operator. Seller has the right but
not the obligation to source from alternate origin. Product must meet EU
import specifications and REACH registration requirements.

5. PRICE

Price: US $470.00 per metric ton DAP delivery point. This is a fixed price
inclusive of all freight, insurance, and transport costs to the named place.

EU duty adjustment formula: DAP price divided by 0.9419 for non-duty-free
origins.

6. PORT(S) OF DISCHARGE

Port of Discharge: One safe port, one safe berth, Rotterdam/Europoort area
for transshipment. Final delivery via Rhine barge to Ludwigshafen.
Minimum draft of 12 meters at discharge berth.

7. ARRIVAL DATES

Arrival Dates: During the period March 15 - April 5, 2026.
Seller to give 10 days prior notice of expected vessel arrival.

8. PAYMENT

Payment: By telegraphic transfer within 30 days from delivery date at
Ludwigshafen. Buyer may provide a standby letter of credit in lieu of
prepayment if requested by Seller.

9. LAYTIME AND DEMURRAGE

Discharge Rate and Demurrage: Discharge rate of 400 mt per hour at
Rotterdam. Rhine barge turnaround not to exceed 48 hours.
Laytime shall be calculated per B/L quantity divided by the agreed rate.
Demurrage: $30,000 per day or pro rata. Claims within 60 calendar days
from completion of discharge.

10. CHARTER PARTY

The Charter Party (ASBATANKVOY form) is incorporated by reference.
For the purposes of this agreement, Seller acts as "owner."

11. VESSEL CLASSIFICATION AND ELIGIBILITY

Vessel Classification and Eligibility: Vessel must be classified by an IACS
member society. P&I coverage must be provided by an International Group club.
ISPS Code compliance is mandatory.

12. INSPECTION

Inspection: Seller's certified weight/measurement at loading port shall be
final and binding for invoicing. An independent inspector appointed by
Seller at loading port.

13. INSURANCE

Insurance: Seller shall arrange cargo insurance on an all risk basis for not
less than 110% CIF value. War strike riot coverage included. Insurance to
cover full transit including Rhine barge leg.

14. LETTER OF INDEMNITY

LOI: Seller may discharge without presentation of an original Bill of Lading
against provision of a Letter of Indemnity in P&I Club approved form.
No bank guarantee shall be required.

15. TAXES, FEES AND DUES

Taxes, Fees and Dues: All import taxes, duties, VAT and fees at destination
shall be added to the sale price and are Buyer's responsibility. Export costs
at origin for Seller's account. EU import duty for Buyer's account.

16. COMPLIANCE WITH EXPORT AND IMPORT LAWS

Compliance with Import Laws: The import of ammonia into Germany is not
prohibited. Product is registered under REACH (EC No. 231-635-3).
Safety Data Sheet shall be provided.

17. WAR RISK

War Risk: Costs arising from areas listed by Joint War Committee shall be
for Buyer's account. In case of Main Shipping Routes Closure affecting the
delivery route, both parties shall negotiate in good faith.

18. FORCE MAJEURE

Force Majeure: Neither party liable for failure caused by Force Majeure.
Notification required within 5 days notice. Obligations suspended for the
duration of the Force Majeure event. Rhine navigation closures exceeding
14 days shall constitute Force Majeure.

19. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

Event of Default: Material breach not cured within 48 hours entitles the
non-defaulting party to terminate. Interest on overdue amounts at 2% per
annum above EURIBOR. Right of setoff and netting preserved.

Penalty for late delivery: If Seller fails to deliver within the agreed
arrival window, Seller shall pay Buyer $25.00 per metric ton on the
delayed volume as liquidated damages.

20. EXCLUSION OF WARRANTIES

Disclaimer of Warranties: SELLER MAKES NO WARRANTY, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED,
AS TO MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR ANY PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

21. CLAIMS

Claims: Notice of any claim within 15 days of delivery. All claims
absolutely barred after one year.

22. GOVERNING LAW AND ARBITRATION

Governing Law: This contract shall be governed by English law.
Arbitration: Disputes to be resolved under LMAA terms in London.

23. NOTICES

Notices: All notices deemed to be given when sent via courier, fax, or
e-mail to the addresses set forth herein.

24. MISCELLANEOUS

Miscellaneous: This document constitutes the Entire Agreement. No oral
Modifications are binding. Waiver of one breach is not waiver of another.
Assignment only with consent. Severability applies to all provisions.

25. HARDSHIP

Hardship: If the fundamental equilibrium of this contract is substantially
altered due to unforeseen circumstances, either party may request renegotiation
in good faith.

LONG TERM FOB AMMONIA PURCHASE AGREEMENT

1. PRODUCT AND SPECIFICATIONS

Product: Anhydrous Ammonia (NH3)
Product Specifications:
  Purity: minimum 99.5%
  Water: maximum 0.5%
  Oil: maximum 5 ppm
  Temperature: minus 33 degrees Celsius

2. QUANTITY

Annual Quantity: 180,000 metric tons per year +/- 10% more or less at
buyer's option over the contract period. Individual lifting size approximately
30,000 metric tons +/- 5% at vessel's option. Minimum lifting obligation of
six (6) cargoes per contract year.

3. INCOTERMS

INCOTERMS 2020: FOB Point Lisas, Trinidad and Tobago.

4. SHIPMENTS AND NOMINATIONS

Shipments / nominations: Buyer shall provide annual nomination of expected
quarterly offtake 45 days before each contract year. Within each quarter,
Buyer shall give 21 days prior notice of each vessel nomination. Nominations
and Notices shall be delivered in writing to Seller's operations department.
Loading Dates: As per individual nomination schedule, evenly spread across
the contract year. No more than two liftings per calendar month.

5. PRICE

Purchase Price: US $355.00 per metric ton FOB loading port for Year 1.
Price for Year 2: price will be agreed based on Fertecon and FMB
quarterly Caribbean FOB assessments with a floor of $310.00 per metric ton
and a ceiling of $420.00 per metric ton.

6. PAYMENT

Payment: By irrevocable letter of credit opened in Seller's favor at a first
class international bank. Payment within 30 days from bill of lading date
against presentation of required documents.

7. DOCUMENTS AND CERTIFICATES

Documents required: Full set (3/3) original clean on-board bills of lading,
commercial invoice, certificate of origin, packing list, and certificate of
quality. All shipping documents to be presented to Buyer's bank within 21
days of B/L date.

8. INSPECTION

Inspection: Independent inspector jointly appointed by Buyer and Seller at
loading port. Inspector's certificate shall be final and binding for quantity
and quality determination. Costs shared equally between the parties.
Bill of lading quantity governs invoicing.

9. PRESENTATION AND COOL DOWN

Presentation: Vessel cargo tanks must be clean, dry, and gas-free prior to
loading. Cool down and purging of cargo tanks shall be at buyer's expense.
Ammonia used for gassing of tanks shall be for buyer's account. Time used
for cool down shall not count as laytime.

10. CHARTER PARTY

ASBATANKVOY Charter Party form is incorporated by reference. Buyer is deemed
"owner" and Seller is deemed "charterer" for loading port purposes.

11. LAYTIME AND DEMURRAGE

Laytime: Calculated as B/L quantity divided by an agreed loading rate of
900 mt per hour, all weather days, Sundays and holidays included.
Demurrage: $38,000 per day or pro rata for any part of a day.
Demurrage claims must be submitted within 90 calendar days from completion
of loading.

12. TAXES, FEES AND DUES

Taxes, Fees and Dues: All export taxes, fees and dues at loading port shall
be deducted from the purchase price. Import duties and taxes at destination
for Buyer's account.

13. COMPLIANCE WITH EXPORT AND IMPORT LAWS

Compliance with Export and Import Laws: Each party warrants the transactions
are not prohibited by applicable export/import regulations. REACH pre-
registration maintained. Safety Data Sheet to be provided prior to first cargo.

14. WAR RISK

War Risk: War risk premium for transit through areas listed by the Joint War
Committee shall be for Buyer's account. In the event of Main Shipping Routes
Closure, parties shall negotiate alternate routing or temporary suspension.

15. VESSEL CLASSIFICATION AND ELIGIBILITY

Vessel Classification and Eligibility: The vessel shall be entered in a class
maintained by a member of IACS, and shall carry P&I insurance with a club that
is a member of the International Group. Vessel must comply with ISPS Code.

16. FORCE MAJEURE

Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for non-performance due to
Force Majeure events. The affected party shall notify the other within 5
business days notice. If Force Majeure persists beyond 90 days, either
party may terminate the affected period. Seller's production curtailment
of more than 30% shall constitute Force Majeure.

17. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

Event of Default: Failure to perform any material obligation not cured within
48 hours of notice constitutes default. The non-defaulting party may terminate
and claim damages. Interest on late payments at 2.5% per annum above SOFR.
Right of setoff and netting under this and all related agreements preserved.

Penalty for volume shortfall: If Buyer fails to lift the minimum annual
quantity (net of tolerance), Buyer shall pay Seller $15.00 per metric ton
on the shortfall volume.

18. GOVERNING LAW AND ARBITRATION

Governing Law: This contract shall be governed by English law.
Arbitration: Any and all disputes shall be referred to arbitration in London
in accordance with LMAA terms. Three arbitrators.

19. NOTICES

Notices: All notices and communications shall be deemed to be given when
delivered by courier, fax, or e-mail to the designated representatives.

20. MISCELLANEOUS

Miscellaneous: This contract, together with its annexes and schedules,
constitutes the Entire Agreement between the parties regarding the subject
matter hereof. No Modifications shall be effective unless reduced to writing
and signed by authorized representatives of both parties. No Waiver of any
provision shall be deemed a continuing waiver. Assignment requires prior
written consent of the other party. Severability shall apply if any
provision is found unenforceable.
